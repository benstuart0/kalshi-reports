Fills within the same event (e.g. multiple spread brackets on one basketball game) are correlated — their markouts move together. The naive SE (std/√N) treats fills as independent and overstates statistical significance. The cluster-robust SE groups fills by event and accounts for within-event correlation. The design effect (cluster SE / naive SE) shows how much correlation inflates uncertainty; values > 2 mean the effective sample size is < 25% of the raw fill count. If the worst-markout events are concentrated in a few correlated clusters, the session-wide markout may be driven by one or two bad outcomes rather than a systematic edge problem.
